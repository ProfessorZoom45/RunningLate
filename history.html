Stories That Built Running Late

Feature articles preserving the moments, systems, and people behind the dynasty.

June 12, 2025

The Server Opens and a League Finds Its Home

Running Late began with a Discord welcome and a simple goal: give committed college-football players a dependable place to compete. The first conversations established the league's voice, its community standards, and the fast-moving rhythm that would define every season after it.

That opening day became the foundation for announcements, scheduling, highlights, Game of the Week voting, and the shared record now preserved across this site.

Summer 2025

From Weekly Games to Weekly Culture

Streams, clips, and highlight posts turned ordinary matchups into a weekly show. Ready-to-advance tracking kept the league moving, while Game of the Week voting gave members a reason to follow the biggest games beyond their own schedules.

June 28, 2026

CFB 27 Draft Night Resets the Board

The CFB 27 era began with a public draft order led by Ohio State at the first pick. Thirty-two user programs filled the ACC, Big Ten, BIG XII, SEC, and Independent boards, creating a fresh competitive map without losing the league's established identity.

July 7, 2026

Day 1 Launch Begins the New Dynasty

Running Late officially launched its College Football 27 dynasty with cross-play enabled, Heisman difficulty, and 48-hour advances. Team hubs, coach profiles, schedules, rankings, and league records now give every season a permanent public history.

The Standard

Move Fast, Stay Ready

The Stay Ready system became more than an advance policy. It is the league's operating principle: schedule quickly, communicate clearly, preserve the games, and keep the dynasty moving without losing the stories that make it matter.
